06. Telephone Etiquettes

Master professional telephone communication skills and maintain courtesy in all phone interactions

What is Telephone Etiquettes?

A set of guidelines and practices aimed at maintaining professionalism, clarity, and courtesy during telephone communication. These etiquettes ensure effective and respectful phone interactions in both personal and professional settings.

Basic Rules of Telephone Etiquette

Speaking Guidelines

  • Speak directly into the mouthpiece of the phone or a headset while talking
  • DO NOT eat or chew gum while talking on the telephone
  • DO NOT cover the phone with your hand or put it against your chest to avoid the caller hearing you. Chances are, they will still be able to comprehend what you are saying

Handling Interruptions

  • If you are interrupted or must talk to somebody else in your workplace while you are on the phone, simply ask the caller if they can hold and press the HOLD button
  • DO NOT place the handset in the cradle until you've pressed the HOLD button
  • DO NOT lay the receiver on the desk, without placing the caller on hold (the caller will hear everything being discussed in your office)

General Courtesy

  • Always be courteous

When Answering the Telephone

Answering Best Practices

  • Always try to answer your own telephone whenever possible
  • Always practice answering your telephone within 2-3 rings

Professional Tip: Answering promptly shows respect for the caller's time and demonstrates your professionalism and availability.

Screening Telephone Calls

Is it appropriate to screen phone calls? This is a topic of much debate. In order to promote or strengthen positive public relations, screening calls is not advised. Whenever possible, you should always err on the side of not screening calls.

Appropriate Responses

  • He is indeed in
  • One suitable reaction to a call that you are not screening is, "May I tell him who's calling, please?"

Remember: Building positive relationships through open communication is more valuable than screening calls. Always prioritize professional courtesy.
